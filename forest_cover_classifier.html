Forest Taxonomy

Seven Cover Types

Each 30×30 meter cell of the Roosevelt National Forest is classified into one of seven ecological zones, shaped by elevation, soil type, and microclimate conditions.

Feature Importance

What the Forest Tells Us

Elevation dominates at 26% — each species occupies a distinct altitudinal band. Distance to roadways, fire points, and hydrology form the secondary signal layer.

Confusion Matrix

Prediction Patterns

The diagonal shows correctly classified cells. Spruce/Fir and Lodgepole Pine are most often confused — their elevational ranges overlap in Rawah and Comanche Peak.
